# 06 — Checkout, Orders, Payments

## Rule

Compra rápida cuando se pueda. Cuenta BOOSTR cuando el producto necesite memoria, acceso o seguimiento.

## Guest checkout allowed

Use guest checkout for simple purchases with low risk and low future-access need:

- simple ticket;
- simple merch;
- physical product;
- simple deposit;
- low-risk order;
- basic order that only needs name, phone/email, and shipping/delivery info.

## BOOSTR account required

Require account when identity/history/access matters:

- Fan Passport;
- VIP Unlock;
- Rewards;
- Wallet Pass;
- beat licenses;
- signed contracts;
- downloadable files;
- private content;
- ticket transfers;
- memberships;
- recurring access;
- high-ticket orders;
- warranties;
- post-purchase portal;
- private drops;
- any flow that requires future access or traceable identity.

## Order notifications

When an order comes in, the business/partner should receive:

- email;
- WhatsApp;
- dashboard notification.

Each notification should include:

- order number;
- partner/workspace;
- customer name;
- phone/email;
- exact items/services;
- quantities/size/options;
- payment method;
- payment status;
- delivery/fulfillment instructions;
- next steps.

## Direct payments

For Zelle/CashApp/direct partner payment, do not force BOOSTR rails when the partner’s business reality depends on direct collection.

BOOSTR should still register the sale as:

- partner-collected/direct payment;
- pending confirmation;
- optional evidence upload;
- confirmed by partner/BOOSTR Advisor.

## Smart Checkout future support

- Stripe;
- Apple Pay / Google Pay;
- BNPL via Klarna/Affirm/Afterpay if available;
- deposits;
- direct payment logging;
- abandoned checkout follow-up;
- order states;
- receipts;
- post-purchase page;
- notifications.

## BNPL

BNPL is a high-impact feature because it makes small partners feel like larger brands. It is especially useful for:

- apparel;
- shoes;
- beats;
- licenses;
- premium services;
- ticket bundles;
- deposits/add-ons.

Do not overuse BNPL for very small purchases where it creates unnecessary friction.
