# 13 — Dashboard UX and Copy Rules

## General

- Mobile-first, but never break desktop.
- Visual, not text-heavy.
- Partner-specific, not generic SaaS.
- Human language, not AI/corporate filler.
- No internal notes shown to partners.
- No data overload.
- Only show what the partner will understand/care about.
- Use cards/carrds like a beautiful app board.
- Make next steps obvious.

## Spanish UI

Spanish should be real Spanish, not mixed with random English unless intentionally bilingual.

Avoid in Spanish UI:

- “assets” for public users;
- “checkout” when “pago/compra” is clearer;
- “website” when “página” is clearer;
- confusing phrases like “comprarían claro.”

## BOOSTR Audit rules

- Interactive lead capture.
- Clear questions.
- No repeated selected options.
- Menus must not get stuck.
- All buttons visible.
- Final result should show multiple alerts, at least 3.
- Emojis are allowed/needed because they are visual.
- Final screen should say BOOSTR will contact them soon.
- Diagnosis = automatic signals + human review.

## Partner-specific notes

### 82NGEL

- Red/black, not pink-heavy.
- Y2K/aesthetic.
- Very graphical.
- Low text.
- App-like/mobile-first.
- Friendly language for a 2004-born artist/creator turning audience into money.

### GEMESE

- Black/platinum or elegant white Apple-coded.
- Not red.
- Urban, minimal, sober.
- Avoid AI phrases.
- Use “NOSOTROS NO ELLOS” without comma.
- Main phrase intent: “Aquí están todas las razones por las cuales somos NOSOTROS NO ELLOS. #NNE powered by BOOSTR Labs.”
- Janko/WESTDETRO asset only when the card relates to NONONO, WESTDETRO, or collab.

### RVZ/Rouvssen

- Minimal.
- Rouvssen first.
- RVZ behind the movement.
- La R al frente.
- Vision, aesthetics, street before over-explaining.
