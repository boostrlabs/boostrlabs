# BOOSTR Partner Model

## Partner

A BOOSTR Partner is a person or business accepted into the BOOSTR ecosystem.

A Partner is not a generic user.

BOOSTR should avoid the word “user” in brand-facing language.

## Account creation

There is no open self-service signup in v1.

Accounts are created by BOOSTR Managers after approval.

## Partner types

Initial supported partner types:

- Artist.
- Creator / influencer.
- Producer.
- Barber.
- Beauty business.
- Car seller / broker.
- Local service provider.
- Digital service provider.
- Apparel/store brand.
- Small business founder.
- Restaurant/hospitality later.
- Fitness / coaching later.

## Partner maturity levels

Useful internal maturity levels:

- Idea.
- Early founder.
- Creator with audience but no sales system.
- Small business already selling.
- Established business.
- Autonomous business/product.

## Partner profile

A Partner Profile is not merely a public profile. It is the central record of a business in BOOSTR.

It should include:

- Name.
- Type.
- Industry.
- Owner.
- Contact.
- Entity status / LLC status.
- Current tools.
- Current revenue estimate.
- Current audience.
- Business goals.
- Active modules.
- Smart Link.
- Dashboard/workspace.
- Inventory.
- Leads.
- Payments.
- Rewards.
- Balance.
- Contracts.
- Notes.
- BOOSTR Score.
- Assigned BOOSTR Manager.

## Partner approval philosophy

BOOSTR is selective.

Not everyone should be accepted.

The user described the metaphor as a car finance process:

- Soft Pull: pre-qualification.
- Hard Pull: deep approval process before creating the partner account.
