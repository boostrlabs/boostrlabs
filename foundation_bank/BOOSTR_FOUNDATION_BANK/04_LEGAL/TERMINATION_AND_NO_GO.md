# Termination, No-Go Partners, and Non-Negotiables

## Termination

An agreement can terminate according to its contract/SOW.

Potential reasons:

- Failure to pay.
- Breach of contract.
- Fraud.
- Chargeback abuse.
- Illegal activity.
- Misuse of BOOSTR systems.
- Abuse of support.
- Failure to provide required information.
- Attempt to bypass BOOSTR fees.
- Unauthorized copying/exporting of BOOSTR Core.
- Reputational risk.

## Offboarding

Upon termination, the agreement should define:

- Whether access continues or ends.
- What data can be exported.
- What assets the partner keeps.
- What unpaid invoices remain due.
- What revenue share remains due.
- What happens to pending payouts/refunds/chargebacks.
- Whether Powered by BOOSTR remains on public pages.

## Non-negotiable clauses

- No fraud.
- No illegal use.
- No chargeback abuse.
- No intentional bypass.
- No copying BOOSTR Core.
- No misrepresenting BOOSTR as label/manager/employer.
- No unlimited support without payment.
- No hiding sales that are contractually subject to BOOSTR revenue share.
- No use of BOOSTR for prohibited/high-risk businesses.

## No-go partners for now

BOOSTR should not accept, or should require additional review for:

- Illegal businesses.
- High chargeback-risk businesses.
- Adult content or sexual products/services.
- Gambling.
- Crypto/financial schemes.
- Supplements/medical claims without compliance.
- Businesses requiring complex health/legal/financial compliance.
- Partners with fraud history.
- Partners who refuse transparency.
- Partners who demand unlimited support at low cost.
- Partners likely to damage BOOSTR reputation.
