# Legal Model

This file is not legal advice. It is BOOSTR's internal legal architecture for future attorney review.

## Legal positioning

BOOSTR Labs LLC is a Technology Solutions and Business Infrastructure Services Provider.

## Relationship with partners

Depending on the partner's entity status, the relationship may be:

- Business-to-business service agreement.
- Individual-to-company service agreement.

However, unless explicitly stated in a separate signed contract, BOOSTR is not:

- Employer.
- W2 relationship.
- 1099 contractor relationship by default.
- Talent manager.
- Artist manager.
- Road manager.
- Record label.
- Distributor.
- Talent agency.
- Joint venture.
- Equity partner.
- Financial advisor.
- Bank.
- Lender.

## Required legal documents v1

BOOSTR should maintain modular legal documents:

1. Master Service Agreement.
2. Statement of Work / Order Form.
3. Revenue Share Addendum.
4. Payments Addendum.
5. Marketing/Campaigns Addendum.
6. Platform Terms & Conditions.
7. Privacy Policy.
8. Partner Referral Terms.
9. Data Processing / Data Usage terms if needed.
10. IP / White-label / Export Addendum if needed.

## Contract architecture

The Master Service Agreement defines the base relationship.

Each partner then activates specific modules through a Statement of Work or Addendum.

Examples:

- Smart Link SOW.
- Business OS SOW.
- Revenue Share Addendum.
- Campaign Addendum.
- Payments Addendum.
- Referral Addendum.

This prevents every partner contract from being completely different while still allowing custom deals.

## No automatic ownership or management rights

BOOSTR does not automatically own or control a partner's business, career, music, royalties, brand, social media, audience, contracts, or outside clients.

BOOSTR only has the rights defined in written agreement.

## Governing principles

- Everything revenue-related must be written.
- Every module must define cost, duration, scope, support, and termination.
- Every payment flow must define who is merchant of record, who handles refunds, and who bears chargeback risk.
- Every intellectual property arrangement must define ownership, license, and export rights.
- Every marketing/campaign agreement must define ad spend, management fee, attribution, and data use.
