# Partner Inventory

Partner Inventory is the correct BOOSTR term for what was technically called Product/Service/Offer model.

Every partner has something to sell.

Inventory can include:

- Products.
- Services.
- Packages.
- Bookings.
- Custom quotes.
- Digital files.
- Beats.
- Apparel.
- Music services.
- Video services.
- Beauty services.
- Car leads/vehicles.
- Events.
- Consultations.
- Memberships.
- Gift cards.
- Deposits.

## Why this matters

BOOSTR cannot improve sales unless it knows what the partner sells.

The partner inventory connects:

- Smart Link.
- Deep Links.
- Payments.
- Campaigns.
- Dashboard metrics.
- Insights.
- Revenue share.
- Leads.

## Inventory attributes

Each inventory item should eventually support:

- Name.
- Category.
- Type.
- Price.
- Availability.
- Capacity.
- Monthly limit.
- Delivery policy.
- Payment mode.
- CTA.
- Revenue share eligibility.
- Campaign eligibility.
- Risk level.
- Media/assets.
- Notes.

## Custom by partner

Inventory labels should adapt by business type.

Artist sees beats, releases, features, bookings.
Barber sees services, appointments, products.
Car seller sees vehicles, deals, leads.
Beauty sees services, packages, add-ons.
