# BOOSTR Economics

BOOSTR does not have one fixed way of making money. BOOSTR earns according to the activated module, partner maturity, risk level, available budget, business type, support required, revenue potential, and contract.

This flexibility is a business strength, but it should not be sold publicly as “we accept anything.” Publicly, BOOSTR should present clear products. Internally, the economic structure is modular and case-by-case.

## Revenue streams

BOOSTR may earn through:

1. Startup fee.
2. Setup fee.
3. One-time project fee.
4. Monthly infrastructure fee.
5. Maintenance/support fee.
6. Revenue share.
7. Payment/smart payment link fee.
8. Campaign setup/management fee.
9. Custom development fee.
10. White-label / export / buyout / license fee.
11. Referral/reward/partner network incentives.

## Startup fee

For selected pilot partners, BOOSTR may charge a low startup fee of approximately $30-$50.

Purpose:

- Cover minimal infrastructure costs.
- Help pay for tools.
- Create commitment from the partner.
- Avoid working 100% free.

This fee is not meant to make BOOSTR rich. It is meant to help transition into better tools and paid systems.

## Minimum monthly fee

Where applicable, BOOSTR can charge $19-$49/month as a low infrastructure fee.

Purpose:

- Hosting.
- Domain support.
- Basic maintenance.
- Basic tools.
- AI tools.
- Operational continuity.

## One-time projects

Almost anything BOOSTR creates can be sold as a one-time project if the partner wants only a finished deliverable.

Examples:

- Logo.
- Branding.
- Landing page.
- Website.
- Smart Link.
- Custom OS interface.
- Assets.
- Apparel concepts.
- Presentation deck.
- Digital catalog.
- Business audit.

However, BOOSTR's priority is not only one-time delivery. The higher-value model is ongoing infrastructure and growth.

## Monthly / recurring products

Recurring fees apply when BOOSTR provides ongoing value.

Examples:

- Hosting.
- Support.
- Maintenance.
- Updates.
- Performance insights.
- Partner dashboard access.
- Campaign support.
- Regular meetings.
- Optimization.
- Data tracking.
- Business guidance.

## Revenue share

Revenue share is appropriate when BOOSTR directly helps generate or process revenue.

Applies only to explicitly defined:

- Sales processed by BOOSTR.
- Sales generated by BOOSTR campaigns.
- Products/services created or structured by BOOSTR.
- Offers sold through BOOSTR Smart Link / OS.
- Specific modules defined in the SOW.

Revenue share does not automatically include the partner's entire business.

## Transition from revenue share to monthly fee

A partner may request or be offered a transition to a fixed monthly fee when:

1. The partner has stable revenue through BOOSTR for 2-3 months.
2. The percentage paid to BOOSTR exceeds a reasonable monthly plan equivalent.
3. BOOSTR approves based on risk, support load, modules, and partner behavior.

This protects both sides:

- Partner can optimize costs as they grow.
- BOOSTR can avoid overworking for low-value accounts.
- BOOSTR can maintain fair upside during early growth.

## Case-by-case logic

Different partners require different economics.

Examples:

- Artist with no structure: low startup + revenue share + later monthly.
- Creator with audience but no sales system: low/no upfront + revenue share.
- Barbero/local service: setup + small monthly + small transaction fee.
- Car seller/broker: likely monthly/service fee, not large revenue share on high-ticket deals.
- SOLVE-like autonomous product: monthly, maintenance, marketing, and custom work.
- 82NGEL/JANKO: internal/pilot/venture-style case studies with custom economics.

## Target business vision

BOOSTR does not need thousands of customers. The model can work with 50-100 strong partners, each producing $100-$500/month or more, plus custom work, campaigns, payment fees, and higher-tier systems.

The long-term vision is to create remote income from creativity, strategy, technology, and business infrastructure.
