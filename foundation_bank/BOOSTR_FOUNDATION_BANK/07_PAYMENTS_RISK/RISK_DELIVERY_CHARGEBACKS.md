# Risk, Delivery, Refunds, and Chargebacks

## Core concern

BOOSTR must reduce carding, chargebacks, refunds, and fraud as much as possible.

It is impossible to eliminate chargebacks completely. The goal is to reduce risk, create clear terms, and collect evidence.

## Delivery policies

### Instant Delivery

For products/services that already exist and can be delivered immediately.

Examples:

- Existing beat.
- Digital file.
- Template.
- Download.

Should require lower risk or verification.

### Next-Day Delivery

For products/services that can be created quickly.

Examples:

- Simple design.
- Short edit.
- Quick creative service.

### 72h Standard Delivery

For higher-risk transactions or new customers.

This adds time to reduce carding/chargeback exposure.

### Pay Now / In-Person

For services delivered in person.

Payment/deposit reserves the service.

Evidence can include:

- Check-in.
- Photo/result.
- Signature/confirmation.
- Location/time.
- Customer messages.

## Evidence to collect

Every transaction should store evidence:

- Terms acceptance.
- Timestamp.
- IP/device metadata.
- Payment metadata.
- Delivery proof.
- Digital file delivery logs.
- Customer messages.
- Before/after proof when applicable.
- Service completion proof.
- Refund policy acceptance.

## Refunds and chargebacks

Default order of responsibility:

1. Partner reserve / payout hold.
2. Next payout deduction.
3. Debt from partner to BOOSTR.
4. BOOSTR covers temporarily only if contract allows.

## Payout holds/reserves

For higher-risk payments, BOOSTR should reserve part of funds or delay payout.

Potential reserve ranges:

- 5%-10% low/medium risk.
- 10%-20% higher risk.
- Longer hold for new partners or digital instant products.

## No-refund policy

No-refund policies help but do not eliminate chargebacks.

They must be clearly accepted before payment.

## Risk levels

Transactions should eventually be labeled:

- Low.
- Medium.
- High.
- Blocked.

Risk can consider:

- New customer.
- High ticket.
- Instant delivery.
- Multiple failed payment attempts.
- Billing mismatch.
- Digital goods.
- Partner history.
- Chargeback history.
