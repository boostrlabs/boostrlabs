# Brand and Language

## Brand tone

BOOSTR should feel premium, futuristic, minimal, serious, Apple-coded, and personal.

The company must speak differently to different industries without losing the BOOSTR identity.

A creator should not feel like they are using a corporate CRM. A car seller should not feel like they are using an artist dashboard. A beauty business should not feel like it is using software built for brokers.

## Visual direction

- 95% black/white.
- 5% dark red/wine gradient accent.
- Minimal Apple-coded cards/bubbles.
- Floating panels.
- Clear metrics.
- No clutter.
- Custom cards per partner.
- Custom colors per partner within BOOSTR system.

## Approved metaphors and ideas

- Auto-Tune for business.
- BOOSTR OS.
- Boosting what already exists.
- Human taste and constancy.
- Technology as leverage.

## Language to avoid or use carefully

Avoid calling BOOSTR:

- Manager.
- Managed by.
- Label.
- Distributor.
- CRM.
- Agency.
- Linktree.
- Wix.
- Booksy.

Use instead:

- Partner.
- BOOSTR Partner.
- BOOSTR Manager.
- BOOSTR Workspace.
- BOOSTR Inventory.
- BOOSTR Smart Link.
- BOOSTR Business OS.
- BOOSTR Insights.
- BOOSTR Rewards.
- BOOSTR Balance.

## Artist-friendly language

For artists and creators, avoid cold terms like CRM, pipeline, KPI, analytics unless the partner understands them.

Use language like:

- Career and business booster.
- Performance stats.
- Fans.
- Opportunities.
- Sales.
- What worked.
- What needs improvement.
- Growth cards.

## Business-friendly language

For business owners and car sellers, more direct business terms can be used:

- Leads.
- Deals.
- Inventory.
- Closings.
- Revenue.
- Appointments.
- Conversion.
- Follow-up.
